Welcome to Casa Vida!

We are delighted that you are considering staying in our holiday apartment. On this page you will find information about the apartment itself and its location.

Whether you are an active cyclist/climber/walker, or a keen golfer, or simply like to spend your time on the beach or around the pool, you will have a comfortable and convenient place to rest and re-charge your batteries.

We hope you will agree that Casa Vida is an ideal base for your holiday.

The apartment

Number 39 comprises a living room/diner, kitchenette, bathroom and two bedrooms, one with twin beds and one with a double bed. The living room/diner and both bedrooms open on to the long balcony, which has panoramic views over the town and the beach. There is a single fold-down occasional bed in the living room. The bathroom contains a modern thermostatically controlled shower but no bath.

The living room and both bedrooms have air conditioning/heating.

Access to the apartment for less mobile visitors is possible from street level via a lift. The lift also goes down to the pool level. A short, medium gradient hill of approximately 50m leads from the apartment block to the substantially level sea front, promenade, shops and restaurants.

Location


Google maps calls the street “Carrer Gran Via Rei Sanxo” and Casa Vida can be seen at the beach end of this road.

The apartment is located on the third (top) floor of a small development built in the traditional Mallorcan style. It is located in the quieter part of Santa Ponsa, just two minutes walk from the lovely sandy beach and a range of restaurants. There is a small supermarket within 200 metres. Santa Ponsa's livelier pubs and clubs are a 10 minute walk away along the attractive promenade. It is a pleasant 15 minute walk to Santa Ponsa marina.

Santa Ponsa is approximately 18 miles from Palma airport. It is a 20 minute drive, mainly on the motorway. For those who do not wish to drive, a taxi costs between 35 and 40 Euros each way. Once at Casa Vida Apartments, a car is not essential and there are buses to Palma and other parts of the island.

Out and about

Mallorca offers much more than just sand, sea and sun.

A visit to Palma is well worth while – the Cathedral is an impressive sight as you approach and you will be amazed at the size of some of the super-yachts and cruise ships you pass. A trip on the train to Soller (and then down to the port on the tram, through the orange and lemon trees) is great fun and Valldemossa is another popular tourist spot. Of course there are the water parks nearby (two at Magaluf) for the whole family to enjoy and there are plenty of evening attractions on the island – including Son Amar (expensive but your ticket includes food and drink and a wonderful show) and Pirates.
